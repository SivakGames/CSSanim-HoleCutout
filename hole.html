Mainly posted for my own reference, but there was a time when I was searching for how to do a kind of inverse clip path. Instead of having the circle remain and discard all the content around it, I wanted the opposite: Cut out a hole out in the center and leave the surrounding content. Ultimately, I wanted to do a kind of circular opening animation.

It turns out, the only CSS needed is simply:

mask: radial-gradient(circle XXXpx, transparent 100%, #FFF 100%);

where XXX is the size of the circle. If you want some blur along the circle's edge, change the transparent part's percentage to a lower one.

Anyway, I just slapped this simple demo together to show how to cut out a hole in content. The above content has the ID simply entitled "the-hole" and anything within will get cut out of the circle.
